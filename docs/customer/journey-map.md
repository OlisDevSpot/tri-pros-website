# Customer Journey Map — Tri Pros Remodeling

## Overview

This map traces the full lifecycle of a Tri Pros customer — from first exposure to post-project advocacy. Understanding the emotional state at each stage enables agents and the system to communicate in the right way at the right time.

---

## Stage 1: Awareness

**What happens**: The homeowner encounters TPR — through a telemarketing call, a social media ad, a neighbor referral, or online search.

**Homeowner's emotional state**:
- Skeptical (especially for cold calls)
- Mildly curious if a real pain point exists
- Defensive — expecting to be sold to

**TPR touchpoints**:
- Telemarketer introduces TPR and establishes there's a relevant problem
- Social media ad surfaces the value proposition or offer

**Key outcome needed**: Appointment booked with at least one decision-maker (ideally both).

**Gaps / risks**:
- Homeowner hangs up or ignores before the offer is made
- Only one decision-maker agrees to the meeting
- The wrong homeowner is targeted (no real problem to solve)

---

## Stage 2: Consideration (Pre-Meeting)

**What happens**: The homeowner has agreed to a meeting. They may look up TPR online, discuss with their spouse/partner, or think about their budget.

**Homeowner's emotional state**:
- Curious — "What will they show us?"
- Slightly anxious — "Are they going to try to pressure us?"
- Realistic — forming rough mental budget expectations

**TPR touchpoints**:
- Appointment confirmation (call/text/email)
- Any online presence they encounter (website, Google reviews, social profiles)

**Key outcome needed**: Both decision-makers present at the meeting; homeowner arrives with an open mind.

**Gaps / risks**:
- No-show or last-minute cancellation
- Spouse/partner has strong preconceptions against home improvement contractors
- Homeowner has already anchored to a low price expectation

---

## Stage 3: Evaluation (In-Home Meeting)

**What happens**: The agent visits the home. Discovery, presentation, price, and close attempt.

**Homeowner's emotional state**:
- Engaged (if the agent builds rapport quickly)
- Hopeful — "This could actually solve our problem"
- Skeptical during pricing — "Is this too much?"
- Anxious at the close — "Are we making the right call?"

**TPR touchpoints**:
- Agent (entire meeting — this is the most critical interaction)
- Material samples
- Portfolio / past results

**Key outcome needed**: Signed contract, or a clear commitment to review the proposal with a specific follow-up time.

**Gaps / risks**:
- Only one decision-maker present → "I need to talk to my spouse"
- Agent presents scope before confirming the customer wants it
- Price presented before value is established → sticker shock
- No follow-up time agreed before agent leaves

**For full meeting playbook**: see `sales/in-home-meeting-playbook.md`

---

## Stage 4: Decision (Proposal Review)

**What happens**: The homeowner receives the proposal, reviews it (alone or with their partner), and makes a decision.

**Homeowner's emotional state**:
- Thoughtful — re-examining the numbers
- Uncertain — remembering the excitement of the meeting but now feeling the weight of the commitment
- Sometimes influenced by outside opinions (friend, family, another contractor quote)

**TPR touchpoints**:
- Proposal document (design, clarity, and content matter enormously here)
- Agent follow-up call/text triggered by proposal view notification
- Response to any questions or concerns raised

**Key outcome needed**: Signed contract.

**Gaps / risks**:
- Proposal goes cold because no follow-up happens
- Homeowner gets a competing quote and compares on price alone
- Confusion about scope causes second-guessing
- Spouse/partner who wasn't at the meeting has unresolved objections

**For follow-up strategy**: see `sales/follow-up-cadence.md`

---

## Stage 5: Commitment (Contract Signed)

**What happens**: DocuSign completed — both parties have a signed copy.

**Homeowner's emotional state**:
- Relieved — decision is made
- Excited — this is going to happen
- Mild buyer's remorse risk in the first 24–48 hours

**TPR touchpoints**:
- Confirmation message from agent: "Excited to get started — here's what happens next."
- Project onboarding communication (timeline, point of contact, what to expect)
- Permit confirmation (if applicable)

**Key outcome needed**: Customer feels confident in their decision and knows what to expect.

**Gaps / risks**:
- Buyer's remorse if there is no prompt, warm confirmation
- Confusion about next steps
- Cancellation if the homeowner feels uncertain in the first 48 hours

---

## Stage 6: Production (Project Underway)

**What happens**: TPR crew performs the work. Materials are delivered, installation happens, inspections occur.

**Homeowner's emotional state**:
- Excited initially
- Sometimes anxious mid-project ("Is this normal?")
- Critical — paying close attention to quality and schedule

**TPR touchpoints**:
- Project kickoff call / site walkthrough
- Communication during the project (updates on timeline, material delivery, crew schedule)
- Agent or project manager as single point of contact

**Key outcome needed**: Project completed on schedule, on scope, with no surprises.

**Gaps / risks**:
- Communication gaps cause anxiety
- Scope change requests that weren't anticipated
- Delays without proactive notification

---

## Stage 7: Completion & Advocacy

**What happens**: Project complete. Final walkthrough. Customer evaluates the result.

**Homeowner's emotional state**:
- Proud and satisfied (if the work is excellent)
- Happy to talk about it (if asked)
- Potentially a future customer for other trades

**TPR touchpoints**:
- Final walkthrough with agent or project manager
- Review request (Google, Yelp, or direct testimonial)
- Referral ask: "Is there anyone in your neighborhood you think might benefit from what we did here?"
- Annual check-in (optional — keeps the relationship warm for future projects)

**Key outcomes**:
- Written or video testimonial
- Google/Yelp review
- 1+ referral
- Cross-sell opportunity identified for a second project

---

## Journey Stage Summary

| Stage | Emotional State | Key Risk | Key Action |
|---|---|---|---|
| Awareness | Skeptical | Hang-up before offer | Clear value hook in first 30 seconds |
| Consideration | Curious / Anxious | No-show, one DM present | Confirm both decision-makers |
| Evaluation | Hopeful → Anxious at price | Sticker shock, spouse objection | Nail discovery; present value before price |
| Decision | Uncertain | Proposal goes cold | Trigger-based follow-up on view |
| Commitment | Relieved → Buyer's remorse risk | Cancellation in first 48h | Prompt, warm confirmation |
| Production | Anxious mid-project | Communication gaps | Proactive updates |
| Completion | Proud and satisfied | No referral ask | Always ask before leaving |
