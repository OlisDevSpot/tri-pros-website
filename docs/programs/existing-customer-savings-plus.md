# Existing Customer Savings+ Program — Pitch Script & Deal Structure

**Program ID**: `existing-customer-savings-plus`
**Best For**: Returning Tri Pros customers
**Accent Color**: Violet

---

## Who This Program Is For

Use this program when the customer is a returning Tri Pros homeowner:
- CRM or call notes confirm prior project history at the address
- Customer references the previous project, crew, or experience
- They're calling about a next phase, additional scope, or follow-up project
- They already trust Tri Pros — this is a relationship conversation, not a first impression

The goal of this program is not to re-sell Tri Pros. It's to reward their loyalty, introduce what's new, and make Phase 2 (or 3, or 4) feel like the natural next step.

---

## Qualifying Signals from Phase 1

- "You guys did our kitchen a couple years ago"
- "We loved [crew member's name]'s work"
- "We've been thinking about doing the master bath next"
- CRM shows prior install at this address
- Customer already knows our process and doesn't ask basic qualification questions

---

## Deal Structure

The Savings+ Program provides returning customers with:

1. **20% loyalty discount** on standard labor rates — applied to the full project, not just a line item
2. **Priority scheduling** — existing customers skip the standard 2–3 week backlog and are placed on the calendar within 3–5 business days
3. **VIP warranty extension** — 2 additional years of workmanship coverage beyond our standard 3-year warranty (total: 5 years)
4. **No mobilization fee** — the standard $350 mobilization fee is waived for returning customers
5. **Exclusive material pricing** — access to our preferred-vendor material catalog with 15–30% better pricing than public retail

**Total average savings for returning customers vs. new-customer pricing**: $1,800–$6,500 depending on scope size.

**Financing**: Same 60-month program as all Tri Pros projects. Monthly payment calculated on net-of-loyalty-discount amount.

**Eligibility**: Customer must have had at least one completed Tri Pros project at the property. The Savings+ benefits apply to the next project only (not retroactive to prior work). Benefits are applied when the new project is started within the current program window.

## Step-by-Step Pitch Script

### Step 1: Welcome Back

> "It's great to see you again — and I mean that genuinely. Families who come back are the most important customers we have.
>
> You already know how we work. You've seen the quality. You know our crews show up clean, on time, and do what they say. So today isn't about convincing you of anything — it's about showing you what's available now that wasn't there before."

**What to do**: Reference the previous project specifically if you have the details. "When we did your kitchen two years ago" is more powerful than "from what I understand." Pull up their history before you arrive.

---

### Step 2: What's Changed

> "Two things are different now compared to when you were last with us.
>
> First: we have new supplier relationships — direct accounts with two premium manufacturers that weren't in our catalog before. That's translating to 20 to 30 percent better material pricing on kitchen and bath scopes right now.
>
> Second: we've launched the Savings+ program, which is specifically for families like yours. Loyalty discount, priority scheduling, extended warranty. None of that is available to first-time customers — it's only for people who've already trusted us with their home."

**What to do**: If you have pricing from their previous project, run a quick comparison. Show them concretely what their Phase 2 scope would have cost two years ago vs. today with the loyalty discount.

---

### Step 3: Your Exclusive Benefits

> "Here's exactly what the Savings+ program includes for you:
>
> Twenty percent off standard labor rates on your entire project.
> Priority scheduling — you go on the calendar within 3 to 5 business days.
> VIP warranty extension — 5 years total workmanship coverage instead of 3.
> And no mobilization fee — that's $350 back in your pocket.
>
> These benefits aren't on our website. They're not available at first appointments. They're exclusively for homeowners who've already done business with us."

**What to do**: Itemize each benefit separately. Don't bundle them into a single vague "discount." Each benefit is its own win — let the customer receive each one.

---

### Step 4: What's Next for Your Home

> "Based on what we've done together — and what I know about your home — here's what I think makes the most sense as a Phase 2.
>
> [Reference the logical next upgrade based on Phase 1 discovery and prior project history.]
>
> Every home has a natural upgrade sequence. The work we've already done created the foundation. The next scope is the one that delivers the biggest visible impact relative to investment."

**What to do**: This step is customized based on the conversation. Don't script it too tightly — let it be specific to their home and priorities. The key is to frame Phase 2 as *inevitable*, not optional.

---

### Step 5: The Easiest Yes

> "You've already done this. You know how our crew works. You know the process. You know what the project looks like when it's done.
>
> The paperwork is the same. The process is the same. The quality is the same.
>
> Except this time, you're getting loyalty pricing, priority scheduling, and 5-year warranty coverage on top. This is the easiest yes you're ever going to make."

**What to do**: Let the confidence land. Don't over-explain or hedge. A returning customer doesn't need convincing — they need permission to say yes.

---

## Objection Handling

### "We're not quite ready yet."

> "I completely understand. There's no pressure here — you know us, we know you.
>
> The only thing I'd mention is that the Savings+ benefits have a timing component. The priority scheduling and loyalty pricing apply to projects started within the current program window.
>
> If timing is the issue, let's talk about what the right start date looks like for you and plan around that."

---

### "The price is still more than I expected."

> "Let's look at that. [Break down the scope.] Keep in mind the loyalty discount is already applied — you're seeing a number that's 20 percent below what a new customer would pay for the exact same work.
>
> If the total number is still a stretch, let's look at the financing again. At 60 months, the monthly payment is [X]. Does that work better?"

---

### "We want to get a few quotes."

> "You've already worked with us — you know what our quality looks like. So if you're comparing, make sure you're comparing equivalent quality, equivalent warranty, and equivalent crew.
>
> I'd encourage you to get the quotes. But when you come back, the Savings+ pricing will still be here — because you're already a customer."

---

### "What if we want to change the scope from what we discussed?"

> "Absolutely — the proposal is flexible until you sign. Let's look at the scope together right now and adjust anything that doesn't feel right. The loyalty discount applies regardless of what scope you choose."

## Close Language

For returning customers, the close is less about removing friction and more about logistics:

> "Let's pull up the proposal together and go through it — takes about 5 minutes. Everything we discussed goes in writing."

> "Since you've done this before, you know what the agreement looks like. I'll walk you through it again, but there shouldn't be any surprises."

> "The easiest part of your second project is the paperwork. Ready?"

If there's any hesitation:

> "What would make it easier to move forward today? Tell me what's holding you back and let's see if we can address it right now."
