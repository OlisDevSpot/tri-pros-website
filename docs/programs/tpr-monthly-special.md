# TPR Monthly Special — Pitch Script & Deal Structure

**Program ID**: `tpr-monthly-special`
**Best For**: New customers, any trade
**Accent Color**: Amber

---

## Who This Program Is For

Use the Monthly Special with any first-time customer who:
- Has responded to a specific monthly offer or general interest call
- Has no strong preference for a specific program (no major energy complaint, not a returning customer)
- Is open to "what's available right now" framing
- Wants a complete scope with pricing today

This is the default program. When in doubt, start here.

---

## Qualifying Signals from Phase 1

- "We've been thinking about doing [trade] for a while"
- "We saw your ad / got a call about [offer]"
- No mention of high utility bills or major comfort issues
- No prior relationship with Tri Pros mentioned
- Wants to know "what would something like this cost"

---

## Deal Structure

The Monthly Special is a bundled offer that includes:

1. **Trade scope** selected during the estimate (kitchen, bath, flooring, windows, or combination)
2. **Monthly promotional discount** — typically $500–$2,500 off standard pricing, varies by scope size and current month incentives
3. **Expedited scheduling** — projects starting this month get prioritized install calendar placement
4. **Standard workmanship warranty** — included on all projects

**Financing**: 60-month term available through our lending partner. Most projects fall in the $200–$400/month range. Same-day approval is possible in most cases.

**Expiry**: The monthly promotional pricing expires at end of month. New projects signed after the 1st of the following month do not qualify.

## Step-by-Step Pitch Script

### Step 1: The Opportunity

> "Every month we put together a special package for the families we meet that month. The pricing includes incentives we only offer during that window — and they expire when the month ends.
>
> Right now, in [Month], that package is active. You didn't just reach out at a good time. You reached out at the *perfect* time. Let me show you exactly what that means."

**What to do**: Pull up the specific scope items you've estimated during Phase 1. Frame the monthly promotion as a time-sensitive decision, not a pressure tactic.

---

### Step 2: Our Promise

> "Before we talk numbers, I want to make sure you know exactly who you're dealing with.
>
> We're a licensed California contractor. Fully insured to $2 million. We've been operating in Southern California for 12 years. And every project we do is covered by a written workmanship warranty — not a verbal promise, an actual document you can keep.
>
> If anything we install ever fails due to our workmanship, we come back. No charge. No argument."

**What to do**: Show the license number if asked. If the customer has had a bad contractor experience, acknowledge it here — don't rush past it.

---

### Step 3: This Month's Package

> "Here's exactly what's included. No asterisks, no hidden line items.
>
> [Walk through the scope line by line — materials, labor, timeline.]
>
> This pricing is what we're offering to families we meet in [Month]. Once [month end date] passes, these incentives come off."

**What to do**: Use the proposal-flow app to show them a live quote if possible. Make every item visible. Transparency here is the close.

---

### Step 4: The Numbers

> "Let's talk about what this looks like on a monthly basis.
>
> Most families we work with choose a 60-month financing term. For a project this size, that puts the payment right around [calculated range] per month.
>
> That's less than most car payments. And unlike a car, this is permanent. It adds real value to your home."

**What to do**: Use actual numbers from their specific scope estimate. Don't give vague ranges if you have a real number. Specificity builds trust.

---

### Step 5: Let's Get Started

> "Here's what happens if we move forward today.
>
> We fill out paperwork together — takes about 5 minutes. Our install coordinator calls you within 24 hours to pick your start date. Most projects are scheduled within a week, and most wrap up in 10–14 business days.
>
> The [Month] pricing locks in today. Ready?"

**What to do**: Pause after "Ready?" Let the silence work. Don't fill it. Wait for a yes or an objection.

---

## Objection Handling

### "I need to think about it."

> "Of course. What specifically would you like to think through? The scope? The pricing? The timeline? Let's make sure you have everything you need to make the decision."

Follow up by identifying the real objection. "Think about it" is usually a proxy for a specific concern.

---

### "The price is too high."

> "I hear you. Let's look at what's driving the number.
>
> [Break down the line items.] The labor portion is fixed — that's the professional installation. The material costs, though — I can look at a few different options with you right now and show you what changes if we adjust [specific item]."

Adjust scope if needed. Never discount on the spot. Redirect to financing instead.

---

### "We want to get other quotes."

> "That's completely fair. I'd encourage you to do that. Just know that when you're comparing, you want to make sure you're comparing the same scope — materials, labor, warranty, and timeline.
>
> A lot of what looks cheaper on paper gets expensive when something breaks and there's no warranty behind it. We put ours in writing."

---

### "We're not ready to decide today."

> "No problem at all. The only thing I'd mention is that the [Month] pricing doesn't carry forward — that's just the reality of how we structure the monthly offer.
>
> But I'm not going anywhere. If you want to talk again this week, I can hold your estimate in the system for 7 days."

## Case Study: The Martinez Family — Whittier, CA

**Situation**: First-time TPR customer. Called in response to an April telemarketing campaign. Interested in kitchen remodel.

**Program**: TPR April Special

**How the sale went**:
- Phase 1 (30 min): identified full kitchen scope — cabinets, countertops, backsplash, flooring
- Phase 2 (45 min): walked through the April package step by step
- Signed on day one — locked in $1,400 promotional discount

**Outcome**:
- Kitchen fully installed in 11 business days
- Total project: $18,400 after promotional pricing
- Family referred two neighbors within 6 months

**Quote**: *"We almost waited until next month. Glad we didn't — the April package was exactly what we needed."*

## Close Language

When the customer is ready, use simple, direct language:

> "Great. Let me pull up the proposal and we'll go through it line by line together. Takes about 5 minutes."

> "Perfect. I'll fill in your information right now and we'll lock in today's pricing."

> "Sounds good. The next step is just paperwork — everything we discussed goes into the agreement."

Never make the close feel like an event. It's just the next logical step.
