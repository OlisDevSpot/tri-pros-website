# Lead Magnet Strategy — Tri Pros Remodeling

## What Is a Lead Magnet?

A lead magnet is a "complete solution to a narrow problem" — something so valuable it could be sold, but you give it away free to identify interested prospects. The best lead magnets don't just collect emails — they reveal a problem that your core offer solves, creating a natural progression to the in-home meeting.

**Framework reference**: Hormozi's Lead Magnet Framework from $100M Leads. See `sales/sales-frameworks.md` for the broader lead generation context.

---

## The Three Types That Work for Home Improvement

### Type 1: Reveal Their Problem (Diagnosis)

Give the homeowner an assessment that surfaces a problem they didn't fully understand. This works because home improvement problems get worse and more expensive the longer you wait.

### Type 2: Samples and Trials

Give them a small taste of the outcome they want. In home improvement, this is typically a visual — before/after of a similar home, a design mockup, or an energy savings projection.

### Type 3: One Step in a Multi-Step Process

Solve part of their problem for free. The remaining steps require your service. This creates a natural, non-pushy progression.

## TPR Lead Magnet Concepts

### 1. Free Home Energy Audit (Type 1: Reveal Their Problem)

**What it is**: A structured assessment that diagnoses a home's energy efficiency — insulation status, HVAC age, window type, estimated waste — and produces a personalized savings estimate.

**Why it works**:
- Surfaces a problem they feel every month (utility bill) but can't quantify
- Creates urgency: "You're losing $X/month in energy waste"
- Positions TPR as the expert diagnostician — not the pushy salesman
- The diagnosis reveals exactly which scopes (insulation, HVAC, windows, solar) apply
- Maps directly to the Energy-Saver+ program

**How to deliver**:
- **Digital version**: Online quiz/calculator that asks 5-7 questions (year built, roof age, HVAC age, window type, monthly utility bill, square footage) and generates a savings estimate
- **In-person version**: Part of the in-home meeting — the "walk the home" technique already does this informally

**Landing page requirements**:
- Headline: "How Much Energy Is Your Home Wasting Every Month?"
- 5-7 question form (minimize friction)
- Instant results page with estimated monthly waste and savings potential
- CTA: "Get a free in-home assessment to see exactly where your savings are"

**Follow-up sequence after completion**:
1. Immediate: Email with their audit results + "Here's what this means for your home"
2. Day 1: "Most homes in [their city] built before [year] have the same issues — here's what our customers have done"
3. Day 3: Case study of a similar home with measurable savings
4. Day 7: Offer for a free in-home consultation: "We'll show you exactly where the waste is and what it would cost to fix"

**Meta Ads integration**: This is the ideal lead magnet for the Energy-Saver+ campaign. Ad → Landing page with quiz → Audit results → In-home meeting booking.

### 2. "Is Your Contractor Licensed?" Checklist (Type 1: Reveal Their Problem)

**What it is**: A downloadable checklist that teaches homeowners how to evaluate any contractor — based on TPR's due diligence story (licensing, scope, supervision, communication, office support, proof of performance).

**Why it works**:
- The due diligence story IS the lead magnet — packaged as consumer education
- Every point on the checklist is something TPR does and cheap contractors don't
- The homeowner evaluates other contractors against TPR's strengths without TPR needing to say "we're better"
- Creates the Contrast Effect before the meeting even happens
- Maps to the psychological mechanics in `sales/due-diligence-story.md`

**How to deliver**:
- **PDF download**: Clean, branded one-pager with 6 checkpoints + what to look for + red flags
- **Interactive version**: Online checklist where they can "score" a contractor they're considering

**Landing page requirements**:
- Headline: "Before You Hire Any Contractor — Use This 6-Point Checklist"
- Brief copy explaining why this matters (horror stories without being fear-mongering)
- Email capture for download
- Optional: "Want us to walk you through this in person? Book a free consultation."

**Follow-up sequence after download**:
1. Immediate: Checklist PDF + "Use this to evaluate anyone you're considering"
2. Day 2: "Here's how Tri Pros scores on each of these 6 points" (subtle but powerful)
3. Day 5: Testimonial from a customer who used the checklist and chose TPR
4. Day 10: "Ready to meet the contractor who checks every box?"

**Meta Ads integration**: Works for the Monthly Special campaign or any general awareness campaign. Position as educational content, not a sales pitch.

---

### 3. Monthly Savings Calculator (Type 3: One Step in a Multi-Step Process)

**What it is**: An interactive tool that calculates potential monthly savings based on the homeowner's current utility bill, home age, and equipment. Shows the gap between what they're paying and what they could be paying.

**Why it works**:
- Solves one step of the problem (quantifying the waste) but can't solve the rest (the actual improvements)
- Creates a specific dollar amount the homeowner anchors to: "I'm wasting $X/month"
- The natural next step is: "How do I stop wasting this?" — which is the in-home meeting
- Leverages Loss Aversion: framing inaction as an ongoing monthly loss

**How to deliver**:
- **Web tool**: 3-4 inputs (utility bill, home age, HVAC age, insulation status) → instant monthly savings estimate
- Results page shows: current waste, potential savings, payback period, and a "see how" CTA

**Landing page requirements**:
- Headline: "Find Out How Much You're Overpaying on Energy Every Month"
- Minimal inputs (3-4 fields max)
- Instant results with dollar amounts
- CTA: "See exactly how to eliminate this waste — book a free in-home energy assessment"

**Follow-up sequence after calculation**:
1. Immediate: Email with their savings estimate + "Here's what your home could look like"
2. Day 2: "Homeowners in [their city] are saving an average of $[X]/month after upgrading"
3. Day 5: Offer for free in-home assessment

## How Lead Magnets Connect to the Core Four

| Channel | Best Lead Magnet | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **Meta Ads (paid)** | Energy Audit or Savings Calculator | Quantifiable results drive ad performance; easy to track conversions |
| **Content Marketing (organic)** | Contractor Checklist | Educational content gets shared; positions TPR as authority |
| **Cold Outreach (telemarketing)** | Energy Audit | "We're offering a free home energy audit in your area" is a warmer opening than "Can I schedule a meeting?" |
| **Warm Outreach (referrals)** | Any — customize to the referral source | "Your neighbor just saved $200/month — want to see what your home could save?" |

## Implementation Priority

| Lead Magnet | Effort | Impact | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Energy Audit (digital quiz) | Medium — needs landing page + quiz logic + email sequence | High — directly feeds Energy-Saver+ pipeline | 1st |
| Contractor Checklist (PDF) | Low — repurpose due diligence story into designed PDF | Medium — brand authority + trust building | 2nd |
| Savings Calculator (web tool) | Higher — needs interactive calculator + utility data | High — strong conversion driver | 3rd |

## Measuring Success

| Metric | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| **Lead magnet completion rate** | Is the landing page + form friction too high? |
| **Email capture rate** | Are people interested enough to give their contact? |
| **Follow-up open rate** | Is the email sequence relevant and well-timed? |
| **Meeting booking rate** | Are lead magnet leads converting to in-home meetings? |
| **Close rate from lead magnet leads** | Are these better or worse leads than cold calls / ads? |
