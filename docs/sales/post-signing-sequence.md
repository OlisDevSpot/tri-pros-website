# Post-Signing Reinforcement Sequence — Tri Pros Remodeling

## Why the First 48 Hours Matter

The moment a customer signs a contract is not the end of the sale — it's the beginning of the most fragile period. Buyer's remorse peaks in the first 24-48 hours. The customer goes from the high of deciding to the anxiety of commitment. Their spouse second-guesses. Their neighbor asks "how much did you pay?" A Google search surfaces scare stories.

Without reinforcement, a signed contract can become a cancellation.

**The fix**: Overcommunicate. Every touchpoint in the first 48 hours reinforces that they made the right decision. By the time doubt surfaces, they've already received multiple confirmations that they're in good hands.

**Framework reference**: This is the CLOSER R (Reinforce) step applied to home improvement. See `sales/sales-frameworks.md`.

---

## The Sequence

### Hour 0 — Thank You (Immediately After Signing)

**Channel**: Text or phone call (whichever you used during the meeting)

**Who sends**: The agent who ran the meeting

**Script**:
> "Hey [Name] — just wanted to say thanks again for trusting us with this. You made a great decision. I'll be your point of contact through the whole process. You'll hear from me again tomorrow with a full rundown of what happens next."

**Purpose**: Immediate warmth. The customer should feel like they gained a relationship, not just signed a contract.

---

### Hours 1-2 — Welcome Email

**Channel**: Email

**Who sends**: Automated or agent

**Contents**:
- Thank you and congratulations
- Clear "What Happens Next" section:
  - Permit application (if applicable) — what it is and how long it takes
  - Materials ordering — timeline for procurement
  - Scheduling — when they'll hear about the project start date
  - Point of contact — name, phone, email for any questions
- Warranty summary (remind them what they're protected by)
- Contact information for TPR office

**Purpose**: Eliminates uncertainty. The #1 driver of buyer's remorse is "I don't know what happens now." This email answers that before the question forms.

**Script for "What Happens Next"**:
> "Here's what to expect over the next few days:
> 1. We'll submit the permit application to your city (takes [X] business days)
> 2. We'll order your materials — you'll get a confirmation when they're on the way
> 3. Your project manager will reach out within [X] days to introduce themselves and schedule the installation
> 4. In the meantime, you don't need to do anything. We handle it all."

---

### Day 1 — Project Manager Introduction

**Channel**: Phone call (preferred) or email

**Who sends**: Project manager or production coordinator

**Script**:
> "Hi [Name], this is [PM Name] from Tri Pros. I'll be overseeing your [project type] from here on out. I just wanted to introduce myself, let you know I'm here for any questions, and give you a quick update: [current status — e.g., permit submitted, materials on order]. I'll check in again in a couple of days."

**Purpose**: The customer now has a named person managing their project. This is a trust anchor — they're not just "in the system."

---

### Day 2 — Progress Update

**Channel**: Text or email

**Who sends**: Agent or PM

**Script**:
> "Quick update on your project — [status update]. Everything is on track. You'll hear from us again when [next milestone]. No action needed on your end."

**Purpose**: Even if nothing material has changed, a proactive update signals that things are moving. Silence breeds doubt.

**Examples of status updates**:
- "Your permit application was submitted to [city] yesterday — typically takes [X] business days."
- "Materials are ordered and expected to arrive by [date]."
- "Your project is in the scheduling queue — we'll have a firm date by [day]."

---

### Day 3-5 — Personalized Touch

**Channel**: Text, email, or physical mail

**Who sends**: Agent

**Options** (choose one):

1. **Similar project photo**: "Was thinking of you when I pulled up this project we finished last month — same [trade], similar home. Turned out great. Yours is going to look just as good."

2. **Handwritten card**: Brief note thanking them for their trust. Feels personal in a way that digital communication cannot.

3. **Relevant tip**: "Heads up — while we're working on your [project], you might want to [practical tip related to the scope]. Just a suggestion — we'll handle everything on our end."

**Purpose**: This is the highest-impact touchpoint. By Day 3-5, the initial excitement has faded and doubt may be creeping in. A personalized, non-transactional touch rekindles confidence.

---

### Pre-Production — Scheduling Confirmation

**Channel**: Phone call + email confirmation

**Who sends**: PM or scheduling coordinator

**Script**:
> "Good news — we've got you on the calendar! Your [project type] is scheduled to begin [date]. Here's what to expect on Day 1: [brief description of what happens — crew arrival time, what they'll do first, parking/access needs].
>
> We're excited to get started. Any questions before then?"

**Purpose**: Excitement language. The word "excited" isn't an accident. By the time installation starts, the customer should be looking forward to it — not dreading it.

## The Anti-Cancellation Effect

Each touchpoint in this sequence reduces cancellation risk:

| Touchpoint | What it prevents |
|---|---|
| Hour 0: Thank you | "Did I make a mistake?" (immediate doubt) |
| Hours 1-2: Welcome email | "I don't know what happens now" (uncertainty) |
| Day 1: PM introduction | "Nobody is paying attention to my project" (abandonment) |
| Day 2: Progress update | "Is anything even happening?" (silence = doubt) |
| Day 3-5: Personalized touch | "Maybe I should have waited" (remorse) |
| Pre-production: Scheduling | "When is this actually going to happen?" (frustration) |

By the time any of these doubts form, the customer has already received an answer.

## CRM Workflow Actions

| Timing | CRM Action |
|---|---|
| Signing | Move deal to "Won" in pipeline; trigger post-signing sequence |
| Day 1 | Log PM introduction call; update project status |
| Day 3-5 | Log personalized touch; flag if customer raised any concerns |
| Pre-production | Confirm schedule in system; send automated confirmation email |

## Connecting to the Follow-Up Cadence

The post-signing sequence is a separate track from the pre-signing follow-up cadence:

- **Pre-signing** (`sales/follow-up-cadence.md`): Day 0 → Day 1 → Day 3 → Day 7 → Day 14+ — focused on getting the signature
- **Post-signing** (this document): Hour 0 → Day 1 → Day 2 → Day 3-5 → Pre-production — focused on keeping the signature

Both sequences share the same principle: every touchpoint has a specific purpose, and silence is the enemy.
