# Competitive Advantage — Tri Pros vs. Cheap Contractors

## The Core TPR Differentiator Framework

When a homeowner compares us to a cheaper quote, we compete on four dimensions:

```
S — Security     (you are protected if anything goes wrong)
W — Warranty     (our work is backed after we leave)
C — Craftsmanship (our work is done right the first time)
E — Experience   (we've done this hundreds of times)
```

This is not abstract. Every claim must be substantiated — with license numbers, insurance certificates, warranty terms, and portfolio examples.

## Why Cheap Contractors Cost More in the Long Run

| Risk with Cheap Contractor | Real Cost to Homeowner |
|---|---|
| Unlicensed work | Cannot sell the home; city can require tear-out |
| No general liability | Homeowner liable for accidents on their property |
| No workers' comp | Homeowner sued if a worker is injured |
| No manufacturer warranty | Defective material = homeowner pays for replacement |
| Poor installation | Callbacks, leaks, failures within 1–3 years |
| Out of business | No one to call when something goes wrong |
| Permit violations | Fines, forced removal, failed inspections |

**The question to ask a homeowner considering a cheap bid:**
> "If something goes wrong 18 months from now — and it will — who are you going to call? And who's going to pay?"

## Manufacturer Warranty Pass-Through

When TPR uses certified materials from major manufacturers, the manufacturer warranty transfers to the homeowner. Cheap contractors using uncertified labor or non-preferred materials void these warranties entirely.

Example:
- A GAF-certified roofing installation includes **GAF's System Plus warranty** covering both materials and workmanship.
- An unlicensed installer using the same shingles voids that warranty entirely.

This means the homeowner with the "cheaper" roof has:
- No manufacturer warranty
- No workmanship warranty
- No one to call

## How to Use Portfolio to Neutralize the Price Objection

1. **Pick 2–3 projects from the same trade** the customer is considering
2. **Tell a short story**: "This homeowner was in the same situation — they'd been quoted lower. Here's what we did and what they said afterward."
3. **Show before/after**: Visuals do more than words
4. **Use the result**: "Their energy bill dropped $X/month" or "They got it inspected when selling and it passed with zero issues"

The portfolio isn't decoration — it's proof that we've done this before and done it right.

## The "Apples to Apples" Framing Script

When a customer says they got a cheaper quote:

> "I completely understand — and I'd never ask you to pay more for the same thing. The question is whether you're comparing the same thing. Can I ask a couple of quick questions about that other quote?
>
> Do you know if they're licensed? Are they carrying general liability and workers' comp? What's their workmanship warranty? Are they an authorized installer with [manufacturer name], which is what protects your material warranty?
>
> I ask because a lot of homeowners find out after the fact that the lower price didn't include those protections. When you add the cost of one callback, one leak, or one injury on your property — the numbers flip pretty quickly."

Then let the customer respond. Most don't know the answers to those questions. That silence is the opening.
